Dwadasha Jyotirlinga

Somnath Jyotirlinga - Patan

It is located in Gujarath,with a backdrop of the vast Arbian Sea.According to popular belief, Somnath Temple was built by the Moon God to show gratitude towards Lord Shiva. The temple was attacked and looted multiple times in the past but has since then been restored to its true glory.

Bhramaramba Mallikarjuna Temple - Srisailam

It is located in Andhra Pradesh.Famous as one of the 12 Jyotirlingas, Mallikarjuna Swamy Temple also holds the distinction of being a Shaktipeeth. Perched atop the Nallamala Hills, it is dedicated to Goddess Bhramarambika.

Mahakaleshwar Jyotirling - Ujjain

It is located in Madhya Pradesh,A revered holy site for Hindus, the temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is architected in Chalukya & Maratha styles and houses a Shiva Lingam which is believed to be Swayambhu (self-manifested).

Omkareshwar Jyotirlinga - Omkareshwar

It is located in Madhya Pradesh, Among the twelve jyotirlingas all over the world, Omkareshwar is the fourth highly revered jyotirlinga. Shri Omkareshwar Temple is located on the banks of the holy river Narmada and known for its majestic Sabha Mandal or the meeting hall.

Baidyanath Dham Jyotirlinga - Deoghar

It is located in Jharkhand.It is a popular tourist site in the state of Jharkhand. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this temple complex boasts 21 temples.

Bhimashankar Jyotirlinga - Pune

It is located in Maharastra,Bhimashankar is a revered pilgrimage site where at 1,005 metres.Amidst the hills of Bhimashankar is the source of the Bhima River and the Bhimashankar Sanctuary with an abundant variety of flora, fauna and birdlife.

Arulmigu Ramanathaswamy Jyotirlinga - Rameshwaram

Nageshwar Jyotirlinga - Dwaraka

It is located in Gujarath.It is also known as Darukavana in the Shiv Purana, Nageshwar Jyotirlinga is about 20 minutes from the Dwarka city centre. The name Darukuvana originates from the Sanskrit word for deodar forests.

Kashi Vishwanath Jyotirlinga - Vaaranasi

It is located in Uttarpradesh. The original temple was believed to have been built around 1490 by Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore. However, the current structure of the temple was constructed in 1780 by the Maratha queen, Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore.

Trimbakeshwar Jyotirlinga - Pune

It is located in Maharastra. Nestled on the foothills of Brahmagiri Mountain Range, this 18th century temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Kedarnath Jyotirlinga - Kedarnath

It is located in Uttarkhand.It is Located near the head of Mandakini river and believed to have been built by the Pandavas to please Lord Shiva, Kedarnath Temple is one of the Char Dhams (where Hindus can achieve instant moksha).

Grishneshwar Jyotirlinga - Aurangabad

It is located in Maharastra.It is the smallest Jyotirlinga temple in India. The walls of the temple also have colourful murals depicting Lord Vishnu's many avtaars.
